Real Life Haunted Houses

The Amityville House

Lorraine Warren referred to the Amityville house as the personification of evil.

One of the most famous and infamous haunted houses. In 1974, Ronnie Defeo Jr. killed his entire family in their sleep with a 35-caliber Marlin rifle. He claimed that voices inside the house made him do it. Later, the house was purchased by the Lutz family who claimed evil, supernatural forces drove them from the home. These included flies, green slime, intense cold, and a demon pig they named Jodie.

The Myrtles Plantation

Several guests and a surveyor have claimed to see the ghosts of Chloe the servant girl, and the children she poisoned.

A multitude of murders, bad business dealings, and poisoned children give The Myrtles a highly creepy vibe. Try and spend the night if you dare!

The Hobo Hill House

The Clarks discovered that their basement contained multiple human-sized cages that were rumored to have housed prisoners.

The Clark family only lasted in this house for 7 months. They reported strange smells, banging noises from the attic, electrical issues, and their daughter having night terrors of a man trying to lock her in her room.

The Winchester Mystery House

The Winchester House has 160 rooms and spans a massive 24,000 square feet.

Mrs Winchester was told my a spiritualist medium that the spirits of those killed by Winchester rifles haunted her and that the only way to stay alive was to keep building and adding on to the house to confuse them
